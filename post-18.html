How to Care for Your Custom Wood Furniture Year After Year

Pending Review
SEO Meta Description

Custom wood furniture is an investment — protect it with the right care routine. MDM Design Studio shares room-by-room maintenance tips to keep your pieces looking exceptional for decades.

The day your custom piece is installed is a milestone. But the decades of enjoyment that follow depend on how well you care for it. Good maintenance isn't complicated — it mostly comes down to consistency, using the right products, and understanding the nature of the material you're working with.

Understanding Your Finish First

Care instructions vary based on the finish on your piece, so step one is knowing what you have. An oil or hard wax oil finish is penetrating, low-sheen, and tactile — it requires periodic re-oiling. Catalyzed lacquer is a film finish, more protective, requiring gentle cleaning but no re-application. High-gloss lacquer is similar but requires more frequent wiping to manage fingerprints. A painted finish is lacquer over paint — treat it like lacquer.

If you're not sure what finish your MDM piece has, check your project documentation or reach out to us — we keep records of every finish used.

Daily and Weekly Habits

Dust with a soft, dry cloth. Microfiber cloths work beautifully and won't scratch the surface. Avoid feather dusters, which can leave oils, and abrasive cloths. Wipe spills immediately. Even lacquer-finished surfaces aren't fully waterproof. Standing water or beverages will penetrate over time, particularly at seams and edges. Use coasters and trivets. Heat and moisture are wood's two biggest enemies in daily use. This applies especially to dining tables and kitchen counters in oil-finished wood.

The Kitchen and Bathroom

For lacquered cabinetry: clean with a slightly damp, soft cloth and a small amount of mild dish soap if needed. Dry immediately — don't let water sit on the surface or collect at the toe kick. Avoid all-purpose sprays, abrasive cleaners, and anything ammonia-based. For cabinet hardware: brass develops a natural patina over time — if you want to maintain the original finish, use a brass-specific cleaner periodically. Matte black and brushed nickel hardware can be cleaned with a damp cloth and mild soap.

Dining Tables

For oil-finished tables: re-oil one to two times per year, or whenever the surface starts to look dry or dull. To re-oil: clean the surface thoroughly, lightly sand with 320-grit if needed, apply oil with a clean cloth in the direction of the grain, and wipe off the excess. Let cure for 24 hours before use. For lacquered tables: clean with a soft cloth and mild soap solution. Dry immediately.

Addressing Humidity and Seasonal Movement

Wood moves with the seasons. Even with proper kiln-drying and acclimation, a solid wood piece will expand slightly in humid summer months and contract in dry winter months. This is normal and not a defect.

To minimize movement: maintain consistent indoor humidity between 35%–55% relative humidity. A whole-home humidifier in winter is genuinely valuable for your furniture. Keep wood furniture away from heating vents, radiators, and direct sunlight. Allow air circulation around furniture — don't press pieces flush against walls permanently.

When Something Needs Repair

Minor repairs — small scratches, dents, worn finish in a high-use area — can often be addressed without professional help using the right products. For larger issues — a deep scratch in lacquer, a split seam, damaged hardware — reach out to us. We stand behind the pieces we build.

The furniture we build is designed to live with you for decades. With proper care, a custom piece from MDM Design Studio only gets better with age. Questions about your specific piece? Email us anytime.
