What Your Lead Time Actually Means

And How to Plan Around It

Pending Review
SEO Meta Description

Custom furniture takes time — but how much, and why? MDM Design Studio breaks down what's behind the timeline so you can plan your project, renovation, or gift with confidence.

One of the first questions clients ask us is: how long will this take? It's a fair question, and one that deserves a clear, honest answer — not a vague estimate designed to sound good in the sales conversation.

Custom furniture takes time. That's not a flaw; it's part of what you're paying for. But understanding why it takes the time it does helps you plan better and feel confident throughout the process rather than wondering what's happening on the other end.

The Phases That Make Up Your Timeline

A typical custom furniture project at MDM Design Studio moves through the following phases, each of which has its own time requirement.

Design and Approval: 1–3 weeks. After your initial consultation, our team develops drawings, renderings, and material specifications for your review. Depending on the complexity of the project and how many revision rounds are needed, this phase typically takes one to three weeks. The more decisive you can be about your preferences in the initial consultation, the faster this phase moves.

Material Sourcing: 1–4 weeks. Once your design is approved, we source your specific materials — wood slabs, sheet goods, hardware, specialty components. Domestic hardwoods are generally available within a week or two. Imported hardware, specialty stone, custom glass, or specific wood species can take longer. We factor this in when quoting your timeline and order early to minimize the impact.

Production: 4–10 weeks. This is the build itself — and for most custom projects, it's the longest single phase. Depending on the complexity of the piece, production at our Birmingham studio typically runs four to ten weeks. During this time, your piece is being cut, fitted, assembled, and finished in multiple stages, with quality checks throughout.

Installation or Delivery: 1–2 days. The final phase is getting your piece home. For freestanding furniture, delivery is typically straightforward. For built-ins and complex cabinetry, installation day can range from a few hours to a full day or more depending on the scope.

What Can Affect Your Timeline

Several factors can extend a project's timeline, and we'll always communicate about them proactively. Material availability — certain wood species, hardware brands, or specialty materials can have unpredictable lead times. Design complexity — more complex designs require more production time. Shop schedule — we work with a finite production team and take on a limited number of projects at once to maintain quality. And client response times — every round of design approval that takes a week adds a week to the total timeline. Quick, decisive feedback from clients keeps projects moving.

Planning for Specific Situations

New home construction or renovation: The best time to engage us is at least three to four months before your target installation date — ideally before framing is complete so we can coordinate with your builder and ensure the space is properly prepared.

A special occasion gift: For a piece commissioned as a wedding gift, anniversary gift, or heirloom piece, reach out at least twelve weeks in advance, and sixteen or more if the piece is complex.

Moving into a new home: If you're buying a home and want built-ins ready when you move in, reach out before closing if possible. The design phase can often begin while the transaction is still underway.

The Right Question to Ask

Instead of "how long will this take?", the most useful question is: "What can I do on my end to keep this project moving?" The answer is usually: be decisive in the design phase, respond to proofs and questions promptly, and finalize your space before we need to install.

We're happy to give you a realistic timeline estimate at any stage. Get in touch and tell us about your project, and we'll give you our honest read on what it takes.
