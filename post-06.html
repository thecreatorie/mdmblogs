How to Prepare for Your First Consultation

With a Custom Furniture Studio

Pending Review
SEO Meta Description

Getting the most out of your first custom furniture consultation starts with preparation. MDM Design Studio shares exactly what to bring, measure, and think through before your first meeting.

Your first consultation with a custom furniture studio is one of the most important conversations you'll have in the life of your project. It's where ideas take shape, expectations get set, and the studio decides whether they can truly deliver your vision. Coming prepared makes all the difference — not just for the speed of the project, but for the quality of the outcome.

Here's everything we recommend having ready before your first meeting with us.

1. Know Your Space

Before anything else, know the dimensions of the space you're designing for. This doesn't need to be architect-level precision — we'll take our own detailed measurements before production begins — but having rough dimensions gives us a working framework for the conversation. Know the approximate width, height, and depth of the wall, alcove, or opening you're working with; ceiling height and whether it's consistent; what's on or near the adjacent walls; and photos from multiple angles in good natural light.

If you haven't measured at all, don't let that stop you from reaching out. We can guide you through it.

2. Gather Inspiration

You don't need to know exactly what you want. You need to know the direction you're going — and inspiration images are the fastest way to communicate that. Save photos from Instagram, Pinterest, Houzz, or our own portfolio that resonate with you, even loosely.

The most useful inspiration images are ones that capture something you genuinely want: a material, a color, a proportion, an organizational feature, a mood. Also valuable: photos of things you don't like. "I hate this" is just as useful as "I love this" in a design conversation.

3. Understand Your Function Requirements

Furniture that doesn't work for your life is beautiful disappointment. Before your consultation, think carefully about what the piece needs to do — not just how it should look. What are you storing or displaying? What are the specific dimensions of the items going in? Do you need drawers, doors, open shelving, or a combination? Are there children or pets in the home that affect design decisions? Is anyone in the household tall or petite in a way that affects counter heights or reach?

The answers to these questions shape design decisions in ways that go far beyond aesthetics.

4. Have a Realistic Budget Range in Mind

You don't have to commit to a number upfront, but having a range in mind — even a loose one — allows us to design toward what's achievable rather than presenting a beautiful design that immediately needs to be value-engineered. We'll never judge your budget, and we'll be honest if a project scope exceeds it.

5. Know Your Timeline

Is this tied to a renovation or construction project? Is there a move-in date? A special occasion? Understanding your timeline helps us determine whether a standard production schedule works or whether the project requires special prioritization.

6. Be Ready to Talk About Your Home's Existing Aesthetic

Custom furniture should feel like it belongs to your home — not like it was dropped in from somewhere else. Bring photos of your existing interior if possible, including the specific room the piece will live in. Know the existing finishes: flooring species and color, wall color, trim profile, hardware finishes in adjacent rooms.

Come to your first consultation curious and open, not anxious. The best design conversations are collaborative, and we do our best work when clients feel comfortable telling us what they truly want — even if they can't quite describe it yet. Schedule a consultation and let's start the conversation.
